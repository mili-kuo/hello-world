🍝 Classic Spaghetti Aglio e Olio Recipe

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Servings: 4

Description

Spaghetti Aglio e Olio is a traditional Italian pasta dish that's simple, elegant, and incredibly delicious. With just garlic, olive oil, red pepper flakes, and spaghetti, this recipe proves that the best dishes don't need many ingredients. Perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or impressive enough for guests!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (450g) spaghetti
  • 1 cup (240ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • 8-10 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) reserved pasta water
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1/4 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat.
  3. Add the sliced garlic to the warm oil and cook slowly for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until fragrant and golden. Do not let it brown.
  4. Add the red pepper flakes to the garlic oil and stir for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the drained spaghetti directly to the skillet with the garlic oil.
  6. Toss the pasta with the oil mixture, adding the reserved pasta water a little at a time to create a light sauce that coats the noodles.
  7. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  8. Remove from heat and stir in the fresh parsley.
  9. Divide among serving bowls and top with Parmesan cheese if desired. Serve immediately.

Tips

  • Use high-quality extra virgin olive oil for the best flavor.
  • Don't let the garlic brown—it becomes bitter. Keep the heat medium-low.
  • Reserve pasta water to create an emulsion that helps coat the spaghetti evenly.
  • Al dente pasta is key—overcooked pasta won't hold the sauce well.
  • This dish is best served immediately while hot.
  • For variations, add anchovy paste, capers, or fresh lemon zest.
  • A small pinch of garlic-infused oil can be drizzled on top just before serving for extra flavor.

Nutritional Notes

This classic Italian dish is naturally vegan (without the Parmesan). It's light, flavorful, and perfect for those following a plant-based diet. Each serving is approximately 600 calories.
