University of Missouri (Mizzou)

Founded: 1839 | Location: Columbia, Missouri | Colors: Gold & Black

About Mizzou

The University of Missouri, commonly known as Mizzou, is a public research university and the flagship campus of the University of Missouri System. It is one of the largest universities in Missouri and has a rich history spanning nearly two centuries.

Notable Features

  • Mizzou Tigers: The athletic teams compete in the Southeastern Conference (SEC)
  • Francis Quadrangle: The historic heart of campus featuring iconic buildings and traditions
  • Columns: Five iconic limestone columns that symbolize Mizzou's heritage
  • School of Journalism: One of the oldest and most prestigious journalism schools in the nation
  • Engineering: A top-ranked program attracting students from around the world

Campus Life

With over 30,000 students, Mizzou offers a vibrant campus community with numerous clubs, organizations, and cultural events. The university is committed to academic excellence, research, and student development.

Traditions

Mizzou is known for its spirited traditions, including the Tiger Rag fight song, the M-Club, and the famous "Mizzou Spirit." Students and alumni share a strong sense of pride and connection to the university.
